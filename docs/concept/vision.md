## High concept

As Logan, explore Valewynn, battle and capture original creatures called Aetherlings with crystalline Aether Prisms, and build a team capable of restoring the disrupted Aetherways in a welcoming strategic adventure.

## Target players and platforms

Family-friendly for creature-RPG players, with tactical depth for adults. Primary targets are modern Windows browsers and iPhone Safari as an installable PWA. Keyboard and touch are required for the prototype; controller support is required before release. Sessions should support meaningful progress in 10–30 minutes. Accessibility begins with scalable text, 44 CSS-pixel touch targets, reduced motion, subtitles, and color-independent information.

## Genre and player fantasy

Single-player stylized 3D creature-collecting RPG with an angled/top-down camera. Players become expert explorers and trainers by discovering habitats, building a personal team, mastering tactical relationships, defeating rival trainers, and investigating Valewynn's failing Aetherways.

## Design pillars

1. Discovery with purpose.
2. Readable strategy without required grinding.
3. A personal team drawn from 60–80 distinct Aetherlings.
4. Comfortable play on laptop and iPhone.
5. A wholly original identity.

### Anti-pillars

No copied Pokémon expression, seamless open world, forced online connection, real-money capture mechanics, loot boxes, energy timers, or grind-first progression.

## Experience summary

Explore connected loadable scenes, enter turn-based encounters, use attacks/switching/items/status, weaken wild Aetherlings, and throw non-spherical triangular Aether Prisms to capture them. Captures join the party or reserve sanctuary, gain experience, learn moves, and may evolve. The presentation uses compact, readable environments and optimized 3D models suitable for iPhone WebGL.

## Differentiation and comparables

Pokémon validates the collect/battle/team loop; Cassette Beasts validates an original monster-RPG identity; Temtem validates systemic readability; Monster Sanctuary validates team-building depth. These are pattern references only. No protected creatures, terminology, visual language, interfaces, maps, stories, audio, or branding may be copied.
